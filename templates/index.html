Top Graduate Schools of Electrical Engineering in the US

This webpage is mainly focused on the the best graduate schools for Electrical Engineering. It is designed to answer three questions.
First, what is the geological distribution of these schools? Why does the distribution come in that way?
Second, how do some characterstics of schools vary in different ranking schools of EE?
Third, what quantitive requirements should students meet to enter schools of EE with different ranking?




Geological Distribution of Top 100 Graduate Schools of Electrical Engineering





We can see from the first graph that best EE graduate schools are mainly located along the east and west coast, as well as centered around metropolises like NYC, LA, San Francisco, Boston, etc. This distribution can be attributed to the wealthiness of coastal area, therefore more budgets can be used on supporting education and reasearch.






Trend of Characteristics as Ranking Varies

Due to the limitation of the dataset, I only visualize one characteristic of the school --- research expenditure. We can see from the chart that as ranking grows (which means the school is getting worse), the research expenditure drops. It indicates that in higher ranking schools, more research expenditure are spent on solving problems in EE-related field.






Requirements to Meet for High-Ranking Schools

Due to the limitation of the dataset, I pick up one standard criteria --- GRE quantitive score. It can be concluded that the requirement for GRE-Q score commonly increases as the ranking of school grows higher. For a student targeting top 30 schools of EE, he should normally gets 759 or higher according to the linear estimation.
